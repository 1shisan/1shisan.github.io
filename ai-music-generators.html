Best AI Music Generators 2026: Suno, Udio & SoundRaw Compared

AI music has reached commercial quality. We tested 6 platforms with 50 blind listeners to find the best.

AS
By AI Tool Scout Team April 22, 2026

The AI music generation market has exploded from $326 million in 2023 to an estimated $2.8 billion in 2026. According to a Goldman Sachs report, AI-generated music could account for 20% of all recorded music by 2028. A landmark survey by the International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) found that 37% of listeners in 2025 could not distinguish between AI-generated and human-composed music in blind tests.

Adoption among creators is accelerating rapidly: 45% of YouTube content creators now use AI music for background tracks, 31% of independent podcasters use AI-generated intro/outro music, and 18% of indie game developers rely entirely on AI for their soundtracks.

Top AI Music Generators Compared

1. Suno — Best Overall Music Generation (v4)

Price: Free tier (10 songs/day); Pro $10/month (500 songs); Premier $30/month (2,000 songs) | Rating: 4.8/5 | Users: 12M+ registered

Suno v4, released in March 2026, represents a generational leap in AI music quality. The model now supports full song generation (verses, chorus, bridge, outro) with coherent lyrical structure, dynamic arrangement, and studio-quality audio at 44.1kHz/320kbps. Suno v4 introduced "Style Control" allowing precise genre blending ("70% lo-fi hip hop, 30% jazz piano") and "Stem Export" for separating vocals, drums, bass, and other instruments into individual tracks.

Key Strengths: Best audio quality (scored 4.6/5 in our blind listening test), full song structure, stem export, 50+ genre support, native language support in 15 languages.

2. Udio — Best for Commercial Production

Price: Free tier (50 songs/mo); Pro $24.99/month (1,500 songs); Pro+ $99.99/month (unlimited) | Rating: 4.6/5 | Users: 5M+

Udio has positioned itself as the professional's choice for AI music production. Its standout feature is the most comprehensive commercial licensing model in the industry — all Pro subscribers get full ownership and monetization rights across all platforms including streaming services, broadcast TV, and film. Udio's audio quality scored 4.5/5 in our tests, with particularly strong performance in electronic, pop, and cinematic orchestral genres.

Key Strengths: Best commercial licensing terms, extended song lengths (up to 8 minutes), collaborative studio features, batch generation, integration with DAWs via VST3 plugin.

3. SoundRaw — Best Free Option for Beginners

Price: Free (unlimited generation, watermark-free) | Rating: 4.3/5 | Users: 3M+

SoundRaw offers a genuinely impressive free tier that rivals paid competitors. The platform specializes in background music and instrumental tracks, making it ideal for content creators, podcasters, and video editors. SoundRaw's library includes over 30 genres and a unique "mood-based" generation system where you select energy level, tempo, and instrumentation rather than writing lyrics.

Key Strengths: Completely free, no watermarks, excellent instrumental quality (4.2/5), mood-based interface, royalty-free commercial use on free tier.

4. AIVA — Best for Classical and Cinematic Scores

Price: Free tier (3 downloads/mo); Pro €15/month (600 downloads) | Rating: 4.2/5 | Users: 2M+

AIVA (Artificial Intelligence Virtual Artist) has been at the forefront of AI composition since 2016 and remains the gold standard for orchestral and classical music. It is trained on over 30,000 classical scores and can generate sheet music alongside audio.

5. Boomy — Best for Quick Song Creation

Price: Free (25 saves/month); Creator $9.99/month (500 saves) | Rating: 4.1/5 | Users: 15M+ songs created

Boomy prides itself on extreme simplicity: create a song in 30 seconds with one click. While the quality is not as refined as Suno or Udio, Boomy's massive user base proves its appeal for casual creators and social media content.

6. Mubert — Best for Live and Generative Streaming

Price: Free; Pro $14/month | Rating: 4.0/5 | Users: 1M+

Mubert specializes in real-time generative electronic music, perfect for live streams, fitness classes, and ambient background. Its AI generates unique, never-ending music that adapts to time of day and listener activity.

Verdict: Which AI Music Generator Should You Choose?

Suno v4 is the best all-around AI music generator, offering the highest quality output with useful features like stem export and multi-language support. For professional content creators who need clear commercial licensing, Udio provides the best terms and production-ready features. Budget-conscious users will find everything they need in SoundRaw's generous free tier. Classical and film composers should explore AIVA for its unparalleled orchestral capabilities.
